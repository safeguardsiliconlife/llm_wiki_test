# Depravity Economy

Term used by 404 Media to describe platforms and products that monetise or gamify human suffering, tragedy, and death as engagement mechanics. Extends the critique of the attention economy into territory where the content being optimised for is intrinsically horrifying.

## Examples
- Kalshi and Polymarket allowing financial bets on wars, deaths, and disasters
- [[ForbesPredict]] embedding prediction widgets on mass-shooting coverage
- News aggregation that treats a children's mass killing as a prompt for a poll

## Structural Driver

The depravity economy is partly a symptom of [[AI Media Traffic Disruption]]: as AI answers the factual questions journalism used to answer, publishers lose traffic and experiment with engagement mechanics that keep users on-site longer — even when those mechanics are contextually grotesque.
