# SaaSpocalypse

Informal term for the structural disruption of SaaS businesses by frontier AI labs. The thesis: companies that built large organisations around steady seat-count expansion are now competing against products built by tiny teams inside the very labs that provide their AI inference.

## Core Dynamics

1. **Inference asymmetry** — SaaS companies pay API rates; frontier labs run their best models at near-zero marginal cost internally. The inference provider's product is always cheaper and more capable.
2. **Headcount asymmetry** — A frontier lab team of ~10 can ship a competitive product. A mature SaaS company carries thousands of employees in overhead.
3. **Moat erosion** — Traditional enterprise moats (multiplayer, plugin ecosystems, design system tooling, enterprise SSO) don't protect against the inference provider, only against other SaaS companies.
4. **Non-designer seat risk** — Many SaaS products expanded by selling seats to non-expert users. AI agents that can perform those tasks make those seats redundant.
