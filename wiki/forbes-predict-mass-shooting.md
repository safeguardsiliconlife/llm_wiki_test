# Forbes Prediction Market Gamifies Mass Shooting Story

404 Media article by [[Matthew Gault]] (2026-04-20) reporting that Forbes embedded a [[ForbesPredict]] widget on a news story about the Shreveport, Louisiana mass shooting (8 children killed, ages 1–14, by Shamar Elkins on 2026-04-19). The widget asked readers to bet tokens on whether Congress would pass gun safety legislation by end of 2026.

## What Happened

- Forbes published a story aggregating AP and NYT coverage of the Shreveport shooting
- Directly below a paragraph about the shooter's mental health struggles, a ForbesPredict box appeared: "Congress WILL/WON'T pass new gun safety legislation before 31st December 2026?"
- Predicting Congress will act cost 100 tokens; ForbesPredict assigned it an 18% probability
- First spotted by Molly White (Citation Needed) on Bluesky

## ForbesPredict Mechanics

- Token-based system with no real money (contrast with Kalshi/Polymarket)
- New users start with 800 tokens; daily login bonus of 800 tokens
- "Hints" cost 10 tokens to preview crowd probability before committing 100 to a prediction
- Tokens unlock status and non-monetary rewards within the Forbes ecosystem
- Built in partnership with [[Axiom]]

## Broader Context

ForbesPredict was launched January 2026 explicitly as a response to [[AI Media Traffic Disruption]] — AI platforms answering the questions journalism used to answer, collapsing referral traffic. Forbes CIO Nina Gould: "Our response isn't to chase scale, but to deepen engagement."

Author Gault frames this as an instance of the [[Depravity Economy]]: media desperate for engagement turns tragedy into a game loop. The product offers "gambling without the stakes and the payout" and "news without first-hand reporting."
